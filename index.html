Understanding NDVI

Sparse/Stressed Moderate Dense/Healthy

Normalized Difference Vegetation Index (NDVI) is a satellite-derived metric used to quantify vegetation health.

It works by measuring the difference between near-infrared light (which vegetation strongly reflects) and red light (which vegetation absorbs).

Significance: It allows early detection of crop stress, disease, uneven irrigation, and nutrient deficiency before they are visible to the naked eye.
